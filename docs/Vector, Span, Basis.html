Linear Algebra: Vectors, Span, and Basis

Linear Algebra: Vectors, Span, and Basis#

Recap from Previous Video#

  • Described vector addition, scalar multiplication, and vector coordinates.

  • Emphasized the back-and-forth relationship between pairs of numbers and vectors.

  • Explored the xy-coordinate system.

Thinking about Vector Coordinates#

  • Each coordinate as a scalar that stretches or squishes vectors.

  • Special vectors in the xy-coordinate system: i-hat (unit vector in x-direction) and j-hat (unit vector in y-direction).

  • Framing coordinates as scalars that scale basis vectors.

Basis Vectors#

  • Basis vectors: i-hat and j-hat in standard coordinate systems.

  • Any pair of linearly independent vectors can form a basis for a coordinate system.

  • Choices of basis vectors lead to different coordinate systems.

Linear Combinations and Span#

  • Linear combination: Adding together scaled vectors.

  • Linear combinations of i-hat and j-hat result in a span covering all 2D space.

  • Span of vectors: Set of all possible vectors achievable through linear combinations.

  • The concept of span becomes more interesting in three-dimensional space.

Span in 3D Space#

  • Span of two vectors in 3D space: Forms a flat sheet.

  • Adding a third vector: Can either sit on the span or unlock access to all 3D space.

  • Linearly dependent vectors: One is redundant, and removing it doesn’t reduce the span.

  • Linearly independent vectors: Each adds a new dimension to the span.

Basis of a Space#

  • Technical definition: A basis is a set of linearly independent vectors that span a space.

  • Puzzle: Why does this definition make sense? Think about the relationships between span and linear independence.

Next Steps#

  • In the next video, explore matrices and transforming space.

  • Matrices play a crucial role in linear algebra, connecting concepts and operations.

Conclusion#

  • Linear algebra involves fundamental operations: vector addition and scalar multiplication.

  • Basis vectors and span provide insight into coordinate systems and vector spaces.

  • Understanding linear dependence and independence is crucial in defining a basis.

  • Matrices will be explored in the next video, contributing to the transformation of space. See you in the next lesson!
